# Say Goodbye to Messy Bookmarks: Learn the Easy and Quick Way to Import Them to Google Chrome!

Are you tired of having messy bookmarks in your browser? Do you want to organize them in a more convenient and efficient way? If yes, then you are in the right place. In this article, we will guide you through the easy and quick way to import your bookmarks to Google Chrome. 

Import bookmarks mean that you can transfer your bookmarks from another browser or device to Google Chrome. It makes it easier for you to access your favorite websites, important links, or web pages. Before starting, ensure that you have exported your bookmarks from a web browser or device. 

Without further ado, here are the steps to follow: 

## Step 1: Open Google Chrome and Go to Bookmark Manager

The Bookmark Manager is where you can add, edit, and delete your bookmarks. You can access it by clicking the three-dot icon in the top-right corner of the browser and choose "Bookmarks" then "Bookmark Manager" from the dropdown menu. 

<img src="https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2016/11/18/21/42/website-1839183_960_720.png" alt="Google Chrome Bookmark Manager" width="500" height="300">

## Step 2: Click on the Three-Dot Icon and Choose "Import Bookmarks"

In the Bookmark Manager, click the three-dot icon again, and select "Import Bookmarks" from the dropdown menu. 

<img src="https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2016/11/18/21/42/website-1839183_960_720.png" alt="Import Bookmarks" width="500" height="300">

## Step 3: Select the File and Click "Open"

A window with the title "Open" will pop up, allowing you to browse and select the file you want to import. Choose the file with your bookmarks, and click "Open" to start the importing process. 

<img src="https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2016/03/31/18/31/document-1298855_960_720.png" alt="Open File" width="500" height="300">

## Step 4: Wait for the Bookmarks to Be Imported

The importing process may take some time, depending on the size of the file and the number of bookmarks. You can monitor the progress of the importing process at the bottom of the screen. 

<img src="https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2019/05/26/12/29/loading-4235710_960_720.jpg" alt="Importing Bookmarks" width="500" height="300">

## Step 5: Enjoy Your Imported Bookmarks!

Once the importing process is complete, you can access your imported bookmarks by clicking the three-dot icon, selecting "Bookmarks," and choosing the bookmarks bar, bookmarks menu, or other bookmarks folders where you want to store them. 

<img src="https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2016/11/18/21/42/website-1839183_960_720.png" alt="Access Bookmarks" width="500" height="300">

In conclusion, importing bookmarks to Google Chrome is an easy and quick way to stay organized and efficient. By following the simple steps outlined above, you can import your bookmarks from other browsers or devices, making it easier for you to access and manage them in just a few clicks. So, say goodbye to messy bookmarks, and hello to a more streamlined browsing experience with Google Chrome.

##   How to Import Bookmarks to Chrome  
 

If you have some old bookmarks archived in an HTML file, here's how to import them to Chrome:

 
- Select the menu (three-dot) icon in Chrome.
 - Choose Bookmarks > Bookmark manager.
 - On the Bookmarks page, select the menu (three-dot) icon, and choose Import Bookmarks.
 - Navigate to the HTML file on your hard drive and select Open. Chrome imports the contents of the file.
 - The imported bookmarks should now appear in the bookmark manager.

 
##   How to Import Bookmarks From Internet Explorer or Edge  
 

Chrome extracts bookmarks and other browsing data (like stored passwords and form data) directly from Internet Explorer or Edge without using an import/export file.

 

Select the menu (three-dot) icon in Chrome.

 

Choose Bookmarks > Bookmark manager.

 

On the Bookmarks page, select the menu (three-dot) icon, and choose Import Bookmarks.

 

Navigate to the HTML file on your hard drive and select Open. Chrome imports the contents of the file.

 

The imported bookmarks should now appear in the bookmark manager.

 
- Open Chrome and select the menu (three-dot) icon.
 - Choose Settings.
 - Under the You and Google section, select Import bookmarks and settings.
 - From the drop-down menu, select your browser and choose the items to import, such as browsing history, favorites, passwords, search engines, and form data.
 - Select Import to begin the data transfer.
 - A Success! message indicates the import process completed correctly.
 - Select Done to close the window and return to Chrome.
 - You can find the imported bookmarks on the bookmarks bar in their respective folder, such as Imported From Edge.
