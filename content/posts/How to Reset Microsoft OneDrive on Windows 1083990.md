Unleash the Secret to Resetting Microsoft OneDrive on Windows 10 and Revitalize Your Cloud Experience!

Microsoft OneDrive is one of the most widely used cloud storage solutions that let you access your files and folders from anywhere in the world. With OneDrive, you can store your documents, pictures, videos, and other digital files securely in the cloud and share them with anyone with ease. However, at times, you may encounter issues with your OneDrive account, such as syncing errors, missing files, or slow performance. If you're facing such problems, don't worry, there's a secret method that can help you reset your OneDrive and give your cloud experience a fresh start.

In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to reset OneDrive on Windows 10 and unleash its full potential. Before we start, we recommend that you backup all your OneDrive files to an external hard drive or another cloud storage service to avoid any data loss.

Step 1: Sign Out of Your OneDrive Account

The first step of resetting OneDrive is to sign out of your current account. To do so, right-click on the OneDrive icon on your taskbar and select "Settings." In the "Account" tab, click "Unlink OneDrive" and confirm your action.

Step 2: Clear OneDrive Cache

After signing out of your OneDrive account, you need to clear the OneDrive cache to avoid any conflicts when you sign in again. To do so, type "run" in the Windows search bar and press enter. In the "Run" dialog box, type "%localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe" /reset and click OK. This will delete all the cached information associated with your OneDrive account.

Step 3: Delete OneDrive Folders

Now, you need to delete the local OneDrive folders that were created when you signed in to your account. To do so, open the file explorer and navigate to "C:\Users\UserName\" (replace 'UserName' with your Windows login name). Here, you'll find the OneDrive folder. Right-click on it and select "Delete."

Step 4: Restart OneDrive

The final step is to restart OneDrive and sign in to your account again. To do so, search for "OneDrive" in the Windows search bar and click on "OneDrive - Desktop app" to launch it. Follow the instructions to sign in to your account and set up the sync settings. You should see a fresh and empty OneDrive folder on your computer, ready to sync your files and folders from the cloud.

In conclusion, resetting Microsoft OneDrive on Windows 10 can be a lifesaver if you're experiencing issues with your account. By following the above steps, you can clear any conflicts, delete local OneDrive folders and cache, and start afresh with your cloud storage experience. Remember to backup your files and folders before resetting OneDrive to avoid any data loss. With a fresh OneDrive account, you can enjoy fast and efficient cloud storage and collaboration seamlessly!

## Steps to Reset Microsoft OneDrive on Windows 10


So, in this article, we will share a detailed guide on how to reset Microsoft OneDrive on Windows 10 to fix sync problems. Let’s check out.

 
### 1. Restart the OneDrive


Sometimes, a simple restart can fix many problems. So, before trying any other method, make sure to restart the OneDrive application first.
To restart OneDrive, you need to right-click on the OneDrive icon on the system tray and select the Close OneDrive option.

On the Confirmation pop-up, you need to click on the Close OneDrive option once again. Then, to relaunch the app, you need to open Windows 10 search and type in OneDrive. Next, open OneDrive app from the search results.
That’s it! You are done. This is how you can restart OneDrive on your PC to fix sync problems.

 
### 2. Reset Microsoft OneDrive


If restarting Microsoft OneDrive didn’t work out for you, then you might need to reset Microsoft OneDrive. Resetting OneDrive is pretty easy. Just implement some of the simple steps shared below.
1. First of all, press Windows Key + R button on your keyboard.

2. Now, you need to enter the path of the OneDrive executable file, followed by /reset in the Run dialog box.
You can locate the OneDrive.exe path in the file explorer. However, the file path may vary depending on various reasons. So, you need to try these commands below.

 

%localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset
C:\Program Files\Microsoft OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset
C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset



You need to execute the above commands one by one. If the command is incorrect, you will receive an error message. Therefore, you need to try every three commands to find the correct one.

3. After entering the command on the RUN dialog box, press the Ok button.
That’s it! You are done. This will reset the Microsoft OneDrive app on your Windows 10 computer.

 
### 3. Reinstall OneDrive Application


If OneDrive is still unable to sync your files, the only option left is to reinstall the OneDrive application.
For that, you need to open the Control Panel > OneDrive. Right-click on the OneDrive app and select Uninstall.

Once uninstalled, you can follow this guide to install the OneDrive application again on your system.
