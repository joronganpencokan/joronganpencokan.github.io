## What is ratatouille?

Before we dive into the recipe, let's talk a little bit about what ratatouille actually is. Ratatouille is a French stew made with a variety of vegetables, including eggplant, zucchini, peppers, onions, and tomatoes. The vegetables are typically cooked low and slow, allowing them to become tender and flavorful. The dish is traditionally served as a side dish, but can also be turned into a main course by serving it with rice or pasta.

## The recipe

Now, onto the recipe itself! Here is everything you will need to make the famous Disney Dreamlight Valley Ratatouille:

- 1 large eggplant
- 1 large zucchini
- 1 large yellow onion
- 1 red bell pepper
- 1 yellow bell pepper
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 2 cups of chopped tomatoes
- 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Olive oil

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
2. Slice the eggplant and zucchini into rounds and set aside.
3. Cut the onion and peppers into thin slices.
4. In a large oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven, heat a few tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat.
5. Add the onions and sauté until they become translucent and begin to soften.
6. Add the garlic and sauté for an additional minute, stirring constantly.
7. Add the peppers and continue to sauté for a few more minutes, until they begin to soften.
8. Add the chopped tomatoes, thyme, and oregano to the skillet and stir everything together.
9. Reduce the heat to medium and allow the mixture to simmer for 5-10 minutes, until the tomatoes have broken down and the mixture has thickened slightly.
10. Remove the skillet from the heat and layer the sliced eggplant and zucchini on top of the tomato mixture, alternating between the two.
11. Season the dish with salt and pepper, and drizzle a few more tablespoons of olive oil over the top.
12. Cover the skillet with a lid or aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven.
13. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the dish is bubbling.
14. Remove the dish from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving.

## Tips for success

To really make this dish shine, there are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind:

- Use high-quality vegetables. The flavor of the dish really depends on the quality of your ingredients, so choose the freshest and most vibrant vegetables you can find.
- Slice your vegetables evenly. This will help them cook evenly and create a beautiful, layered presentation.
- Don't skimp on the seasoning. Ratatouille is a simple dish, but it relies on the flavors of the vegetables and herbs to really come to life. Be generous with your seasoning.
- Use an oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven. This will allow you to transfer the dish directly from the stovetop to the oven, which makes the cooking process much easier.
