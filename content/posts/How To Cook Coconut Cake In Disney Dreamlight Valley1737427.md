Unveiling The Secret Recipe To Make Lip-Smacking Coconut Cake Like The Pros in Disney Dreamlight Valley

Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour
2 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
1 cup granulated sugar
3 large eggs
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/4 teaspoon almond extract
1/2 cup milk
1 cup sweetened shredded coconut

Frosting:

1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
4 cups powdered sugar
1/4 cup heavy cream
1 teaspoon coconut extract
1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
1/2 cup sweetened shredded coconut

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Line 2 9-inch cake pans with parchment paper.
2. Combine the flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside.
3. In a large mixing bowl, cream the butter and sugar until pale and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, and beat well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla and almond extract.
4. Add the flour mixture and milk alternatively to the butter mixture, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined.
5. Fold in the shredded coconut.
6. Divide the batter between the prepared pans and smooth the tops. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the centre comes out clean.
7. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Turn the cakes out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

Frosting:

1. In a large mixing bowl, beat the butter until smooth.
2. Add the powdered sugar, heavy cream, coconut extract, and salt. Beat until smooth and pale.
3. Add the shredded coconut and mix until well incorporated.
4. Frost your cooled cake layers and decorate as desired.

Tips:

- For a more pronounced coconut flavour, you can add coconut milk instead of regular milk.
- Toasting shredded coconut adds a delicious aroma and depth to the cake. Simply spread the coconut in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake at 350°F for 8-10 minutes or until golden brown.
- Make sure your butter and eggs are at room temperature before starting to bake. This ensures a smooth and even batter, resulting in a more tender cake.
