In today's digital age, email is an essential communication tool for both personal and business use. However, with more and more data breaches happening every day, it's crucial that you take steps to protect your email address. The consequences of a data breach can be severe and can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and even reputational damage. In this article, we'll explore the best ways to keep your email address safe and secure.

**Use a strong, unique password**

The first step in protecting your email address is to use a strong, unique password. Your password should be a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols, and it should be at least eight characters long. Avoid using easily guessable passwords such as your birthday, your pet's name, or "password." Also, refrain from using the same password for multiple accounts, as this makes it easier for hackers to gain access to all your accounts at once.

**Enable two-factor authentication**

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is an extra layer of security that requires you to input a code or use a fingerprint or facial recognition in addition to your password to access your email account. This makes it much harder for hackers to gain access to your account, even if they have your password. Most email providers offer 2FA as an option, so be sure to enable it as soon as possible.

**Be cautious of suspicious emails**

Phishing emails are a common way that hackers try to gain access to your email account. These emails are designed to look like they come from a legitimate source, such as your bank or an online store, but they actually contain a link or attachment that, when clicked on, installs malware on your computer or prompts you to enter your email credentials. To avoid falling prey to phishing emails, only click on links and open attachments from trusted sources, and never provide personal or sensitive information in an email request.

**Regularly update your software**

Ensuring that your operating system, browser, and email software are up-to-date with the latest security patches and updates is a simple, yet effective way to protect your email address. These updates are designed to fix security vulnerabilities that could be exploited by hackers, so it's essential to stay on top of them.

**Use a virtual private network (VPN)**

If you frequently use public Wi-Fi networks, such as those at coffee shops or airports, it's essential to use a virtual private network (VPN) to protect your email address. A VPN creates a secure, encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet, which makes it much harder for hackers to intercept your data. Many VPNs also include additional security features that can help keep your email account and other online activities safe.

In conclusion, protecting your email address from data breaches should be a top priority. By using a strong, unique password, enabling two-factor authentication, being cautious of suspicious emails, regularly updating your software, and using a virtual private network when using public Wi-Fi networks, you can significantly reduce your risk of falling victim to an email data breach.

## Have I Been Pwned
 
Have I Been Pwned is the most popular site for email and password monitoring, having been in existence since 2013. To use the site, you enter your email, and the site will list any breaches your email has been part of.
 
The site does offer its premium 1Password service to reduce your chance of having it compromised. It provides a variety of plans for individuals, families, and businesses beginning at $36.00 per year.
 
## BreachAlarm
 
When you put in your email address, BreachAlarm sends you an email with the results. It also asks for verification that you are indeed the owner of the email. You have the option to activate their Email Watchdog for free.
 
## Firefox Monitor
 
Firefox Monitor displays your information concerning breaches right on the screen after you enter your email. The site offers a way to get notified if your email is compromised again. Create an account, and you can monitor multiple email addresses for free. I also found this list to be the most comprehensive. It listed breaches the other sites did not.
 
## Inoitsu Email Address Breach Analysis
 
The Inoitsu Email Address Breach Analysis site is plain and simple. Put in your email and find out if it’s been compromised. Your results are listed on the page. You don’t need an account, nor do you have to check your email to get your information.
 
Also found on the home page is a list of recent breaches that you can scan through to look for any that may concern you or your loved ones.
 
## Password Checkup by Google for Chrome
 
Password Checkup by Google for Chrome is not a site where you need to enter your email. It is an extension that alerts you of breaches that have occurred on a site you visit. When you try to log in to a website that has had a data breach, the extension will automatically notify you that you need to change your password.
 
## Avast Hack Check
 
Put your email address into Avast Hack Check, and you receive an email they send to that address. This email shows the breaches where your information could have been compromised. You must use the email link within 24 hours, or it will be invalid.
 
## Identity Leak Checker
 
When you enter your email into Identity Leak Checker, it sends you an email message with the information concerning breaches of your data. You receive a chart that clearly shows what exactly has been compromised, whether it was a password, your name, or your address.
