Unlock Endless Streaming Possibilities: The Ultimate Guide to Installing Kodi on Android!

Kodi has become popular due to its ability to stream movies, TV shows, and other media content effortlessly. However, before you can exploit its full potential, you need to know how to install it on Android. This article is an ultimate guide that lets you unlock endless streaming possibilities on your Android device by guiding you on how to install Kodi on Android. 

What is Kodi?

Firstly, it is essential to understand what Kodi is. Kodi is a media player software that allows you to stream a variety of content, such as TV shows, movies, and live sports events, from various sources globally. Kodi’s popularity can be attributed to its ability to work as a multi-purpose software that can run on various operating systems, from Windows and iOS to Android.

Preparing Your Android Device for Kodi Installation

Before installing Kodi on your Android device, you need to ensure that your Android device is up-to-date with the latest updates and has enough working storage space available. Kodi is a large application that requires more storage space; therefore, ensure you have at least 100MB of storage on your Android device.

After verifying the storage requirements, install the latest version of Kodi on your device. Before you can install Kodi, you need to enable ‘unknown sources’ in your device’s settings by navigating to “security” and enabling “Unknown Sources.” This allows you to install apps that are not on the Google Play Store on your Android device.

Installing Kodi on Android

Now that you have prepared your Android device for Kodi installation, follow the steps outlined below to make Kodi available on your Android device:

1. Navigate to the Kodi download page and download the Kodi APK file for the Android operating system.
2. After downloading the Kodi APK file, navigate to the downloaded file’s location on your Android device.
3. Tap on the Kodi APK file, then select the “Install” button to initiate the installation process.
4. After the installation is complete, select “Open” to open Kodi on your Android device.

Configuring Kodi on Android

You have now successfully installed Kodi on your Android device. However, to enjoy a smooth streaming experience, it is essential to configure Kodi’s settings on your Android device. Here are some essential settings tweaks that you can undertake on Kodi:

1. Customize the user interface to your desired theme by navigating to “Settings” and then to “Interface Settings.”
2. Establish a remote control setting by navigating to “Settings” and then to “Service Settings” to allow control of Kodi on your Android device.
3. Configure the add-ons settings by navigating to “Settings,” then “Add-ons,” then “Manage Dependencies” to optimize Kodi’s functionality.

Conclusion

You have now successfully installed and configured Kodi on your Android device! Kodi is a powerful media streaming application that allows you to watch your favorite TV shows, movies, and live sports events on your Android device for free. The guide above has provided you with enough information to install Kodi on your device step-by-step. Harness Kodi’s full potential and get access to endless streaming possibilities. Happy streaming!

## How to install Kodi on Android
 
Installing Kodi on your Android phone or tablet is as easy as it gets. You can use any of the two methods to make it work.
 
Method 1
 
For this method to work, you need to install a few apps on your device – LocalCast, any good file manager ( like Solid Explorer or ES File Explorer and of course Kodi. Install them all. Now, you will have to download a .xml file with the name playercorefactory.
 
After you’ve downloaded the things you need, using the file explorer of your choice, enable the option to see hidden files. You can find the option to do so on your file explorer’s settings. After that’s been taken cared of, go to the folder containing the .xml file and copy it to Android > Data > org.xbmc.kodi > files > .kodi > userdata.
 
You’re now good to go. Open Kodi and play any media file. Select LocalCast as the app to use and select Chromecast. You should now be able to play the video on the TV that the Chromecast is plugged into.
 
Method 2
 
This is the easier method to make Kodi work. This method uses the screen mirroring feature of the Google Home app. All you have to do is open the menu > tap on Cast Screen / Audio > Select the Chromecast device > open Kodi and play any video. You’ll have your device’s screen mirrored on the screen that Chromecast is connected to.
