Title: Unlock the Secret to Freeing Up Storage Space on Your Android - Learn How to Identify the Sneaky Apps Hogging all Your GBs!

If you're an Android user, then you're well aware of the struggle of constantly running out of storage space on your phone. It can be frustrating when you want to take a photo or download a new app, but you're met with the dreaded "out of storage" notification. One solution to this problem is to identify the apps that are taking up the most space on your phone and deleting them. In this article, we'll show you how to do just that.

Step 1: Check Your Storage

The first step in identifying the apps that are taking up the most space on your Android phone is to check your storage. To do this, go to your phone's Settings app and click on "Storage." This will show you how much storage space is being used and how much is available.

Step 2: Sort Your Apps by Size

Once you know how much storage space you have left, the next step is to sort your apps by size. This will enable you to identify the apps that are taking up the most space on your phone. To do this, tap on "Apps" in the Storage menu, and then click on "Sort by size." This will show you a list of all your apps, ordered from largest to smallest.

Step 3: Check App Storage Details

Now that you've sorted your apps by size, click on the app that's taking up the most space. This will open up a page with further details on that app's storage usage. Here, you can see the amount of space taken up by the app itself, as well as any files or data associated with it.

Step 4: Clear App Cache and Data

If you find that an app is taking up a lot of space on your phone, you can try clearing its cache and data. This will not delete the app itself, but it will remove any temporary files and data that it has accumulated over time. To clear the cache and data for an app, go back to its storage page and click on "Clear cache" and "Clear data."

Step 5: Delete Unnecessary Apps

If you find that an app is taking up a lot of space on your phone, and you don't use it frequently, consider deleting it altogether. To delete an app, go back to your app list and click on the app you want to delete. Then, click "Uninstall" and confirm the action.

In conclusion, identifying the apps that are taking up the most space on your Android phone is a simple but effective way to free up storage space. By following these steps, you'll be able to identify the sneaky apps hogging all your GBs and take action to clear space on your phone. With a little bit of effort, you'll be able to enjoy a phone that runs smoothly, with plenty of storage space for your needs.

## 2 Methods to See Which Apps are Taking up Space on your Android


So, in this article, we will share a step-by-step guide on how to see which of your installed apps on Android is using the most storage. Let’s check out.

 
### 1) Using the Phone’s Storage Manager


The Android phone offers a built-in storage manager that can be used to check the space used by photos, videos, documents, and apps. Here’s what you need to do.
1. First of all, open the Settings app on your Android smartphone.

2. On the Settings, scroll down and tap on the Storage option.

3. On the Storage, tap on the Apps.

4. On the next page, you will be able to see which Android apps are taking up the most space.

Important: The Storage management option may vary from phone to phone. Usually, the option is available on the Settings app.

 
### 2) Using Google Play Store


Google Play Store has a feature that allows you to manage apps installed on your device. You can use that feature to see which apps take up the most space on your Android phone.
1. First of all, open the Google Play Store on your Android smartphone.
2. On the Google Play Store, tap on your profile picture.

3. tap on the Manage Apps & Device option on the next menu.

4. On the next screen, switch to the Manage tab, as shown in the screenshot below.

5. Now, you will see the list of apps. To sort apps by size, tap on the Sort menu and select ‘Size‘

6. Google Play Store will now display the apps based on their size.

That’s it! You are done. This is how you can use Google Play Store to see which Android apps are taking up the most space.
