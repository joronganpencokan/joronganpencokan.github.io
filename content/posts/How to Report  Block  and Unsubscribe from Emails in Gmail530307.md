# Say Goodbye to Email Spam for Good! Learn the Ultimate Guide to Reporting, Blocking, and Unsubscribing from Emails on Gmail Today!

Email spam is one of the most annoying things that people have to deal with nowadays. It clogs up our inboxes and distracts our attention from the important emails we need to read. Fortunately, there are several ways to deal with email spam on Gmail.

In this article, we will provide you with the ultimate guide to reporting, blocking, and unsubscribing from emails on Gmail. By following these simple steps, you can say goodbye to email spam for good and finally achieve inbox zero!

## Reporting Spam Emails

One of the best ways to deal with spam emails is to report them to Gmail. This will help Gmail learn to recognize spam emails and automatically filter them out of your inbox. Here's how to report spam emails on Gmail:

1. Open the spam email you'd like to report.
2. Click the three-dot icon in the top right corner of the message.
3. Select "Report spam" from the drop-down menu.
4. A pop-up message will appear asking: "Report this message as spam?" Click "Report" to confirm.

## Blocking Senders

Blocking senders is another effective way to prevent spam emails from flooding your inbox. Blocking a sender will automatically direct their emails to your spam folder or prevent them from reaching your inbox altogether. Here's how to block senders on Gmail:

1. Open the email from the sender you want to block.
2. Click the three-dot icon in the top-right corner of the message.
3. Select "Block [sender's name]" from the drop-down menu.
4. A pop-up message will appear asking: "Block [sender's name]?" Click "Block" to confirm.

## Unsubscribing from Emails

If you receive newsletters or promotional emails that you no longer want, it's important to unsubscribe rather than just deleting them. By unsubscribing, you're letting the sender know that you're no longer interested, and it also ensures that you're not accidentally marked as spam in the future. Here's how to unsubscribe from emails on Gmail:

1. Open the email from the sender you want to unsubscribe from.
2. Scroll to the bottom of the email and look for the unsubscribe link.
3. Click the unsubscribe link.
4. You may be taken to a confirmation page to confirm your unsubscribing request. Follow the on-screen prompts to confirm.

## Additional Tips to Keep Your Inbox Clean

Here are some additional tips to keep your inbox clean and free of spam:

1. Use filters to automatically sort your emails into folders.
2. Don't share your email address with untrusted sources.
3. Be cautious with your email subscriptions and don't sign up for anything you don't truly want.

In conclusion, spam emails are a nuisance, but there are ways to deal with them effectively. By following this guide, you should be able to report, block, and unsubscribe from unwanted emails like a pro! So go forth and enjoy an inbox that's free of spam!

### 1) How to Block Emails in Gmail?


You can use the web version or the Gmail mobile app to block emails. Here’s how you can block emails in Gmail for desktop & mobile. Let’s check out.

 
### Block Emails in Gmail (Desktop)


1. First of all, open your favorite web browser and head to Gmail.com. Next, log in with your Gmail account.
2. Now, open the Spam email that you want to block. Next, click on the three vertical dots as shown in the screenshot below.

3. From the list of options that appears, click on the Block ‘sender’s name’.

That’s it! You are done. This is how you can block emails in Gmail for desktop.

 
### Block Emails in Gmail (Mobile)


1. First, open the Gmail app on your Android or iOS device.

2. Now, open the Spam email that you want to block.
3. Next, tap on the three dots displayed behind the title.

4. From the list of options that appears, tap on the Block ‘sender’s name’.

That’s it! This will block the email address on your Gmail account. You are done.

 
## 2) How to Report Emails in Gmail


If someone sends you an email with malware attachments or phishing webpages, it’s best to report them. Here’s how to report Emails in Gmail.

 
### Report Emails in Gmail (Desktop)


1. First of all, open your favorite web browser and head to Gmail.com. Next, log in with your Gmail account.
2. Now, open the Spam email that you want to block. Next, click on the three vertical dots as shown in the screenshot below.

3. From the list of options, click on the Report Spam.

4. If the email contains phishing links, you need to tap on the three dots and click on the Report Phishing option.


 
### Report Emails in Gmail (Mobile)


1. First, open the Gmail app on your Android or iOS device.

2. Now, you need to open the Spam email you want to block.
3. Next, tap on the three dots displayed on the top bar.

4. Now, from the list of options that appears, tap on the Report Spam.

That’s it! You are done. This is how you can report emails in Gmail.

 
## 3) How to Unsubscribe from Email in Gmail


We often subscribe to many web services and apps and receive emails from them. These emails were legit, but you might not be interested in receiving those emails. In that case, you can choose to unsubscribe emails in Gmail. Here’s how you can unsubscribe from email in Gmail.

 
### Unsubscribe Emails in Gmail (Desktop)


1. First of all, open your favorite web browser and head to Gmail.com. Next, log in with your Gmail account.
2. Now, open the email that you want to unsubscribe.
3. Next, click on the small unsubscribe text next to the sender’s name.


 
### Unsubscribe Emails in Gmail (Mobile)


1. Open the Gmail app on your Android or iOS device. Next, open the email that you want to unsubscribe.
2. Now scroll down to the end of the email body, and tap on the Unsubscribe button.
3. Alternatively, tap on the three dots on the top bar.

4. Next, tap on the Unsubscribe button.

That’s it! You are done. This is how you can unsubscribe from emails in Gmail.
